A Review on Nest Mini

Last month Google sent me a Nest Mini as a part of the giveaway program for premium Youtube users. The Nest Mini, Google's newest smart speaker, features an assortment of improvements like stronger sound, faster processing to deliver a better user experience than its predecessor. During the setup process, it pairs with the Google Home application, connects to your home wifi, and integrates itself with your Google account for personalized assistance.

Technical Specifications

Dimensions
  • Weight: 181 g
  • Height: 42 mm
  • Diameter: 98 mm
  • Power cable: 1.5 m
Power
  • DC power jack
  • 15 W power adapter
Wireless Connectivity
  • 802.11b/g/n/ac Wi-Fi
  • Bluetooth 5.0
  • Chromecast
Processors
  • Quad-core 64-bit ARM CPU 1.4 GHz
  • High-performance ML hardware engine

Design and Interaction

The Nest Mini has a rounded shape with high-quality fabric design. It comes in four color choices: chalk, charcoal, coral, and sky. The Mini has a dedicated microphone off slider switch on the side, so you can disable the microphone anytime. The volume controls are two capacitive touch buttons located on the sides of the device along with embedded LEDs indicating volume levels.

Voice Assistant

The voice assistant can be activated through the "hey google" command. Google added a machine learning chip with the Nest Mini which allows the device to process commands locally. It can deliver personalized information to multiple people in the home, supporting up to six peopleâ€™s voices using voice matching.

Sound and Microphone

The Nest Mini has a 360-degree sound and 40mm speaker driver along with improved audio tuning software which provides a balanced music experience. It can automatically adjust its volume based on the background noise in the room. When it comes to voice detection, the Nest Mini is top-notch. It hears voice commands even in the noisiest of situations.
